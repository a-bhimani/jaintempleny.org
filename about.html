• The Jain Temple of New York (JTNY) is a non-profit religious organization dedicated to practicing, promoting and preserving the Jain Religion in the New York metropolitan area.

    Prathistha

    The Prathistha (consecration ceremony) of three idols was performed from May 22-30, 2010, marking a significant milestone in the temple's history.

    Mission

    JTNY serves as a spiritual home for the Jain community, offering a place for worship, religious education, and cultural preservation. The temple organizes various events and activities throughout the year, welcoming dignitaries from India and around the world for lectures and discourses on Jainism.

  • The JTNY Constitution establishes the governance structure, membership guidelines, and operational procedures for the organization.

    Key Provisions

    • Purpose: To practice, promote, and preserve the Jain Religion
    • Membership: Open to individuals and families who support the temple's mission
    • Governance: Board of Trustees and Executive Committee
    • Elections: Regular elections for board and committee positions
    • Amendments: Constitution may be amended by member vote

    For a complete copy of the constitution, please contact the temple office.
